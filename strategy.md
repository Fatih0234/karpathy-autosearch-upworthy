# Headline Winner Prediction Strategy

## Core Hypotheses

### 1. Curiosity Gap
Headlines that create an information gap — implying surprising or unknown information — drive higher CTR. Key signals: "secret", "revealed", "you didn't know", "real reason", "what really happened".

### 2. Specificity & Numbers
Specific numbers increase credibility and set concrete expectations. "7 ways" outperforms "some ways". Years, statistics, and named entities add specificity.

### 3. Emotional Resonance
Words that trigger strong emotions (positive or negative) increase engagement. High-signal words: "devastating", "inspiring", "shocking", "beautiful", "heartbreaking".

### 4. Question Format
Questions engage readers by making them want to know the answer. Works best when the question implies a surprising answer.

### 5. Optimal Length
Headlines between 60-100 characters hit the sweet spot: long enough for context, short enough to scan. Too short = vague; too long = overwhelming.

### 6. List Format
"N things/ways/reasons" headlines set clear expectations and feel scannable. Boosts CTR especially for numbers 5, 7, 10.

## Tie-Breaking Rules
When scores are close, prefer:
1. The headline with a stronger emotional word
2. The headline with a specific number
3. The shorter headline (easier to process)
